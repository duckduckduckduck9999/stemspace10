Egg Drop Challenge

Updated 2025-08-23 â€¢ STEM for Kids Space

Protect a raw egg from breaking by designing a contraption that slows impact.

Materials

  • Raw eggs
  • Tape
  • Straws
  • Paper
  • Balloons
  • Cotton
  • Plastic bags
  • Box for drop zone

Steps

  1. Brainstorm designs that increase time to stop and distribute force.
  2. Build the device; keep egg removable for testing.
  3. Drop from a safe height into a box or tarp.
  4. Iterate based on results; document what changed and why.

Whatâ€™s happening (the science)

  • Impulse equals force Ã— time. Increasing stop time lowers peak force.
  • Parachutes increase air resistance; crumple zones absorb energy.

Safety & cleanup

  • Use a tarp; avoid drops near people.
  • Wash hands and surfaces after egg handling.
  • Clean up broken shells promptly.

Tip: Extend this activity by asking students to measure, graph, or compare versions (e.g., different designs or variables).
